Lok Lak

About: Lok Lak

Lok Lak is a popular dish that is prevalent in Cambodian and Vietnamese cuisine.
It is believed to be inspired by the French, originating in the early to mid 1800s.

Lok Lak consists of beef cut into cubes and marinated with a blend of savory sauces and finished off with a lime, salt, and pepper dressing.
Though not required, it is commonly served with a fried sunny side up egg on top.

Ingredients

  • 900 grams of beef
  • 4 table spoons of oyster sauce
  • 2 table spoons of sugar
  • 4 table spoons of minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons of tuong gia vi soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • Romaine lettuce
  • 1 Sliced Tomato
  • 1 green onion
  • 1 lime
  • Fish sauce
  • Ground Black Pepper

Steps

  1. Cut beef into cubes
  2. Mix together the 4 table spoons of oyster sauce, two tablespoons of sugar
    two table spoons of fish sauce, 4 table spoons of minced garlic,
    two tablespoons of thick soy sauce, and one teaspoon of black pepper.
  3. Add cubed beef into the mixture and marinate for at least two hours.
  4. In a separate smaller bowl, add one minced garlic clove, juice from lime
    and salt and pepper to taste. A pinch of sugar is optional.
  5. Wash the lettuce and pat it dry, then lay the leaves flat on a plate.
  6. Slice the tomato and lay it on top of the lettuce.
  7. Heat two table spoons of oil on a pan then add the marinated beef and cook fully
  8. When fully cooked, turn off the heat and allow the beef to rest for five minutes in the pan.
  9. Remove the meat from the pan and place it on top of the plate of lettuce and tomato.
  10. Pour the lime juice mixture from the separate smaller bowl from earlier over the meat.
  11. Garnish with cilantro and green onion before serving.
